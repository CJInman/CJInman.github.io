About Me

My name is Christopher Inman and I have recently been awarded my PhD in astrophysics from the University of Lancashire. I work in the radiative transfer modelling of spiral galaxies. My doctoral thesis involved implementing a perscription for quiescent clumps into the radiative transfer models of Popescu et al. 2011. This provided a solution to the long standing energy balance problem faced by the radiative transfer community.

Having recently completed my PhD and a postdoctoral contract, I am keen to build on this expertise while remaining open to new research directions and collaborations.

Research Interests

  • Radiative transfer in galaxies
  • Multiwavelength galaxy analysis
  • Computational astrophysics
